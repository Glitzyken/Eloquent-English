LINKING & SILENT LETTERS

LINKING IN PRONUNCIATION

Linking in pronunciation is a way of joining the pronunciation of two or more words to flow smoothly.

Native speakers of English do link their words naturally. At times, because of the way he is linking his words, you might want to strain your ears a bit more to hear what a native speaker is saying.

In order for you to flow when speaking English, you need to learn how to link your words. As a learner, failing to link words naturally or deliberately in spoken English may result in awkward pauses or extra, unnecessary sounds.

Linking is very important in English. If you recognize and use linking when speaking, people will understand you better when you are speaking and vice versa.

There are two widely understood accent of English:

  • General American (GenAm)
  • Received Pronunciation (RP). It is also called the BBC English, Queens English, Oxford English, etc.


The Americans are rhotic speakers of English - they do pronounce the sound of letter “R” at the beginning and end of words.

Whereas, the Received Pronunciation is non-rhotic. Letter “R” is only sounded at the beginning of words. This is where linking in pronunciation occurs.

The rules of Letter “r” in British English are:

  • Letter “r” is sounded at the beginning of words like rat, ring, robe, Ruth, etc.
  • Letter “r” is sounded when it is followed by a vowel or a semi-vowel. We have words like foreign, forever, lorry, print, pray, etc.


Linking “r” occurs when the last word ends with letter “r” and the next word begins with a vowel in a phrase or sentence.

I’m going far away from here. (The pronunciation becomes fa-raway)

It is a matter of fact (matte-rof)

My car is bad (ca-ris bad)

Murder is a crime. (mude-ris)

Consonant to Vowel Linking

This linking occurs when the last consonant in a word is linked to the nearest vowel. Instead of pronouncing the word in isolation, you just link the words in order to flow when speaking.

Here are some sentences with consonant to vowel linking.

She has an apple
She ha - san - na - pple

Can I have a bite of egg?
ca - ni - ha - va - baɪ - to - vegg?

That is good
Tha - tis - good

Vowel to Vowel Linking

When a word ends with a vowel sound and the next word begins with a vowel sound, we link with the sound /j/ which is letter “y” or /w/ which is letter “w”. The linking to /j/ or /w/ depends on the shape of your mouth at the end of the first word.

Linking vowel to vowel with /j/

If the first word containing a vowel ends with a neutral sound or with wide lips, as in a /eɪ/, e /i: /, /aɪ/, you link with the /j/ sound to the next vowel.

Study the phrases and sentences below.

Three eggs
Three yeggs /θri:jegs/

I like to eat tea and bread
tea yand bread /aɪ laɪk tə i:t ti:jənd bred/

The bee is busy
bee yis /ðə bi:jəz bɪzi/

Linking Vowel to vowel with /w/

When the first word ends with o /əʊ/, u /u:/ sounds, the lips are round. During the linking, we insert /w/ sound at the beginning of the next vowel of a word.

Study the phrases and sentences below.

Go out now
Go wot now /gəʊ waʊt naʊ/

She comes here too often
She comes here too wofen /ʃi: kʌms hɪə tu:wəfən/

You are always late
You ware always late /ju:wə ɔ:lweɪz leɪt/

I want two eggs
I want two weggs /aɪ wənt tu:wegz/

For you to flow well when speaking English, you need to practice linking to words. Be intentional and conscious of what you are saying. You do not have to rush what you are saying. Make sure effective communication still take place when linking words.

We have discussed lots of things that can help you become an eloquent English speaker, but we still need to work on silent letters. A lot of people do pronounce debt by adding the ‘b’ instead of /det/ and ‘gnash’ as /gnæʃ/ instead of /næʃ/. This is because they are not aware that in words, some letters are not pronounced.

This leads to what silent letter is and the rules of silent letters.

WHAT IS A SILENT LETTER?

Silent letters are letters which appear in a word but are not pronounced during speech.

We have two types of silent letters.

Auxiliary silent letter

The auxiliary silent letters work with other letters to form a specific sound that doesn’t sound like either of the letters in the silent letter pair.

Example of these are sh, ph, th.

While Endocentric auxiliary silent letter combinations produce the sound of one of the letters in the pair. Double consonants such as ‘ff’ in the word, puff, ‘ss’ in the word hiss. In those words, only one of the consonant’s’ and ‘f’ is produced.

Dummy silent letters

Dummy silent letters are simply letters that are not produced. The dummy silent letters are silent in words for the following reason.

The silent letter may actually be pronounced when the base word changes to a longer word. For example, in the word ‘sign’, the ‘g’ is not pronounced, but in the word ‘signature’, the ‘g’ makes a sound. These are called inert letters. If the letters in words do not change to make a sound, then, they are known as empty letters.

Now that you have known what silent letters are, it is important to know the rules of silent letters for accurate pronunciation of words.

Rules of Silent Letters

Silent ‘b’

Letter b is silent before t as in debt, doubt, subtle B is also silent when it comes after m as in bomb, comb, dumb, thumb, tomb, climb, and womb.

Silent ‘c’

Letter c is silent when it follows the letter’s’ and comes before ‘I’ and ‘e’, For example, we have science, scepter, scent.

C is also silent before ‘k’. We have duck, mock, luck.

Letter c is also silent when it comes before letter ‘z’ in a word. We have czar, Czech, czarina.

Letter c can also be silent before letter ‘L’ in a word. Take a look at the word MUSCLE /’mʌsl/ not /mɒskl/.

Silent ‘d’

D is silent when it appears before the letter N and G. We have words like Wednesday, grudge, cadge, pledge.

D is also not pronounced in sandwich, handkerchief, handsome.

Silent ‘e’

E is silent at the end of words. The vowel before the ‘e’ makes a long vowel sound. We have words example like cake, fine, hope, Pete, cube.

Silent ‘g’

G is not pronounced when it comes before N in a word. We have the following words with silent ‘G’. Gnash, design, foreign, sign, align, champagne.

G is also not pronounced before letter m in the word diaphragm.

‘Gh’ is not pronounced when it comes after a vowel in a word. Height, though, alight, thought, light.

‘Gh’ is sometimes pronounced like ‘f,’ as in cough, laugh, enough, tough, rough.

Silent ‘h’

H is silent when it appears after letter ‘w’ in words like why, when, where, what.

H is not silent after letter ‘w’ sometimes when the vowel preceding the ‘h’ is letter ‘o’. Like in the word who, whole, whose, whom.

H is silent at the beginning of these words. Hour, honest, honor, heir (always use the article ‘an’ when pronouncing words with silent H).

H is also silent after letter r in the word myrrh.

Silent ‘i’

L is usually not pronounced after the vowels a, o and u. We have half, would, folk, calf, palm.

Silent ‘n’

Letter n is not pronounced when it comes after m as the end of a word. We have words like column, solemn, autumn, and damn.

Silent ‘p’

P is not pronounced at the beginning of many words using the combination PS, PT, and PN.

We have words like psalm, psychology, pneumonia, pterodactyl, pneumatic. Ph is sometimes pronounced like ‘f’. You can listen to the pronunciation like telephone, paragraph, elephant, alphabet, physical.

Silent ‘s’

Letter s is not pronounced before letter L in the following words. Isle, island, aisle, islet.

Silent ‘t’

Letter t is not pronounced in the following words, Castle, Christmas, nestle, fasten, often, apostle, whistle, wrestle, hasten, rapport, depot, ballet, Chevrolet.

Silent ‘u’

The letter u is not pronounced when it comes after G and before a vowel in a word. We have words like guitar, guide, guard, guest, and guess.

Silent ‘w’

W is not pronounced at the beginning of a word when it is before the letter ‘r’. We have words like wrong, write, wrap, wreck, wrestling. W is also silent in the following words like who, sword, two, answer, whole.



(Please beware that there are exceptions to some of the silent letter rules. It is important to check your dictionary when you are confused about the correct pronunciation of a word).
