WHAT IS AN ACCENT?

An accent is a manner of pronunciation peculiar to a particular individual, location or nation. It is the way of pronouncing a language.

Every region and social group have its accent used across the world. Speakers from different regions in a country might have different accent for the same language.

Native language speaker’s accent is different from the foreign language speaker, so, every foreign language learner needs to focus on the accent of the native language speaker to understand him.

THE DIFFERENT TYPES OF BRITISH ACCENT

When you think of the British, you imagine poise, sophistication, and royalty. You can associate English movies that you are accustomed to, men who are impeccably, well–mannered with high grant’s posh accent and the easily comprehensive Queen’s English. In fact, you are not wrong in recalling the English movie, ‘My fair lady’, by Professor Henry Higgins, who always enunciates his words.

The different accents are listed below:

GEORDIE

Geordie normally refers to both the people and dialect of Newcastle-Upon-Tyne in Northeast England. It is one of the oldest and best-loved British dialects, but it is slowly dying off.

SCOTTISH ENGLISH

Scots roll their sound of letter ‘r’ regularly and break their words so that they sound like they have been cut off in the middle. For instance, ‘cot’ instead of ‘caught’ and ‘not’ with ‘nee’. So instead of saying you ‘didn’t do anything in Edinburgh’, it sounds more like ‘didnee do anythin’ in Ednbrah.’

ESTUARY ENGLISH (SOUTHEAST BRITISH)

This accent can be heard around Southeast England, East Anglia, the Midlands, and North. It is slightly similar to General American in the US.

COCKNEY

Commonly spoken in the East End of London is the cockney accent. It is typically associated with working class citizens.

WEST COUNTRY (SOUTHWEST BRITISH)

The West Country accent can be heard in the South of England, just about fifty miles West of London and extending to the Welsh border. Often, the letter ‘r’ is pronounced after vowels. Instead of saying mother as ‘muthah’, someone from the Southwest would say ‘mutherrr’.

MIDLANDS ENGLISH

The most famous is the Brummie English. This accent has made headlines claiming to baffle a computer software. Callers to the Birmingham City Council have been left frustrated because the authority’s computerized phone system cannot understand the Brummie accent.

WELSH ENGLISH

The accent is heavily influenced by the Welsh language. Although a survey has shown that some Welsh feel that their accent may prove to be a hindrance to their career, others remain proud and describe the accent as melodious and lilting.

QUEEN’S ACCENT

When people talk about learning British pronunciation, they usually think of Received Pronunciation (RP) which can also be called the Queen’s English, standard English, BBC English, Posh accent, Oxford English etc. This is the pronunciation you will learn at the British language school. The Received Pronunciation (RP) is a non-rhotic English, which means that the letter ‘r’ is usually silent at the end of words, unlike the American English. In the RP, letter ‘r’ is mostly sounded at the beginning of words. It can only make it sound when it is followed by a vowel (This is called linking r).



To be able to speak using a posh accent, we need to neutralize our accent by avoiding mother tongue interference in our English pronunciation. This requires much effort.

ACCENT NEUTRALIZATION

Accent neutralization which is also known as accent reduction, is an approach for learning or adopting a new speech accent. It is the process of learning the sound system–which is the English Phonology and the melodic intonation of a language in order to communicate with clarity and be understood by everybody.

Improving one’s accent is not a simple task. To have a good accent, you need training and regular practice of the vowels and consonants, have correct intonation and pronunciation.

WAYS TO IMPROVE YOUR ACCENT

  • Listen to the audio reading of an accent you want to imitate.
  • Use Dictionary to check the correct pronunciation of words. There are so many dictionaries online. Make use of it to get the correct pronunciation of any word.
  • Work on your intonation.
  • Practice everything you learn.
  • Have confidence in yourself.
